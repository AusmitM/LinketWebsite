# Linket Backlog

Prioritized from the research report and mapped to the current product.

## Now

### 1. Lead follow-up workflow

- Add persistent notes and reminder timestamps to leads.
- Surface them in the inbox with quick editing.
- Include them in CSV export and dashboard summaries.
- Why first: this is the clearest monetizable pain. Users pay to avoid losing context and to follow up faster.

### 2. Follow-up visibility in analytics

- Count contacts with notes and reminders.
- Highlight leads that need attention soon.
- Why next: analytics becomes useful when it drives action, not just reporting.

### 3. Attribution that explains where leads came from

- Track share method, source URL, and campaign metadata consistently.
- Why next: teams want to know which channel, event, or page variant created the lead.

## Next

### 4. Team and event ROI views

- Break down scans and leads by profile, event, and rep.
- Add filtered leaderboards and exportable summaries.
- Why next: managers pay when the product proves team output.

### 5. CRM and workflow integrations

- Add webhooks, Zapier-style sync, and direct CRM export.
- Why next: this removes copy-paste and keeps the system of record clean.

## Later

### 6. Native A/B testing

- Test CTA order, page variants, and lead-form layouts.
- Why later: it is a strong differentiator, but it matters after the workflow is already useful.

### 7. Privacy-safe analytics mode

- Keep first-party tracking, coarse geo, and consent-aware controls.
- Why later: important for regulated buyers, but it builds on the workflow layer.

### 8. Warehouse/API portability

- Add bulk exports and API access for BI systems.
- Why later: useful for ops-heavy teams once they are already measuring follow-up.
